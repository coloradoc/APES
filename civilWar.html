The Civil War

The American Civil War, fought from 1861 to 1865, was an important turning point in American History arising from deep ideological divisions between Northern and Southern states. The Civil War was caused by debates over slavery and states' rights. The war would change American policy over rights forever. The Union would triumph, leading to the abolition of slavery and a redefined relationship between the federal government and the states.
